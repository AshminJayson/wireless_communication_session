How to use this guide

  • One block per slide. Each block shows the slide number/title, “On screen” (what the audience sees) and “Talk track” (what you say — expand naturally, don’t read it).
  • The anchor slide is #5 “One signal, five generations.” Everything else hangs off the idea that each generation solved the previous one’s hard limit. If you’re short on time, protect that slide.
  • Lean into your edge. You’re a hardware engineer — slides on Massive MIMO/beamforming (#11), the three domains (#13), and roles (#23) are where you can go off-script with real experience from Cavli.
  • Keep the big numbers honest. 20 Gbps, 1 ms, ~1 Tbps are ITU/research targets, not what a phone shows. Saying so builds credibility.
  • Timing: ~25–30 min of content leaves room for Q&A. Part openers (the “Part One/Two…” slides) are 10-second breathers — use them to reset pace.
  • Audience hooks: show of hands on slide #7 (3G download wait); invite their field on slides #14, #19.

05Evolution · The signal · ANCHOR

One signal, five generations

On screen
  • 1G (1980s) — Analog voice. First mobile calls, no data.
  • 2G (1990s) — Digital + SMS. Texting, clearer calls.
  • 3G (2000s) — Mobile internet. Web & multimedia.
  • 4G (2010s) — LTE broadband. HD video streaming.
  • 5G (2020s) — Connected ecosystem. Low latency, massive scale.
Talk track

The deck’s anchor — slow down here. Walk left to right: voice, text, internet, video, machines. Point at the waveforms getting denser across the row — each generation carries more, and a new kind of information, not just more speed. Every later slide refers back to this picture.

095G · Definition

What is 5G?

On screen
  • Fifth generation of mobile networks — an intelligent, connected ecosystem for people, machines, and industries.
  • Objectives: extreme speed & capacity · ultra-low latency · massive device connectivity · mission-critical reliability.
20 Gbps peak data-rate target (ITU IMT-2020) ~1 ms air-interface latency target 10⁶ / km² connected-device density
Talk track

Stress that 5G is a platform, not a faster phone. The three numbers are ITU IMT-2020 targets — ideal, not what a handset shows today. Set that expectation honestly; it makes you more credible, not less.

115G · Air interface

Massive MIMO & beamforming

On screen
  • Massive MIMO (Multiple-Input, Multiple-Output) — dozens-to-hundreds of antennas per cell, serving many users at once, far more capacity & coverage.
  • Beamforming — focused, steerable signals aimed at each user; less waste, better signal strength & efficiency.
Talk track

MIMO = more antennas, more parallel streams. Beamforming = aiming instead of broadcasting — floodlight vs. steerable spotlight. This is your world as a hardware/RF engineer — go off-script here with a real example.

Your edge: spend extra time. This is the most hardware-native slide in the deck.
125G · Architecture & standards

An architecture defined by 3GPP

On screen
  • 3GPP (3rd Generation Partnership Project) — the global body that standardizes mobile networks.
  • Chain: UE → NG-RAN → 5G Core → Data Network, with reference points Uu (air), N2/N3, N6.
  • Release strip: Rel-15 (first 5G, 2018) · NR (new radio) · 5GC · NSA → SA.
Talk track

5G isn’t one vendor’s design — it’s a shared specification, published in numbered Releases (Rel-15 first full 5G in 2018; Rel-16/17/18 add “5G-Advanced”). The arrow labels (Uu, N2/N3, N6) are the actual 3GPP reference points. NSA reused the 4G core first; SA is the full 5G core. Walk it left to right.

135G · The three domains

UE, RAN & 5G Core

On screen
  • UE (User Equipment) — device + 5G modem + (U)SIM; phones, vehicles, cellular IoT modules; speaks NR over the air.
  • NG-RAN — the radio layer; gNB base stations with Massive MIMO/beamforming; split into CU / DU / RU.
  • 5G Core (5GC) — cloud-native, service-based; functions AMF / SMF / UPF; control-plane / user-plane split.
Talk track

UE is your world — the device, modem, and SIM; at Cavli you build cellular IoT modules, which are UE. RAN is the radio: gNB, now split into CU/DU/RU for flexible deployment. The Core is cloud-native and service-based — AMF (access & mobility), SMF (sessions), UPF (moves user traffic). The control/user-plane split is what makes network slicing and edge breakout possible.

176G · The next leap

Why 6G, and what to expect

On screen
  • Why: today’s networks cap out for the most demanding future apps; relentless data growth; immersive, AI-driven experiences 5G can’t fully serve.

Talk track

The headline: 6G isn’t just faster — it’s AI-native with near-zero latency. Frame ~1 Tbps as an aspirational research target, not a committed spec, to keep your numbers credible.

186G · Enabling technologies

What makes 6G possible

On screen
  • AI in the network — self-optimizing, predictive maintenance, autonomous management.
  • Terahertz (THz) communication — very-high-frequency spectrum for ultra-high data rates.
  • Space–air–ground networks — satellites + drones + terrestrial, integrated for global reach.
  • Digital twins — live virtual replicas of physical systems.
Talk track

Four enablers. Terahertz and space–air–ground are the most physically novel — and the hardest hardware problems (your wheelhouse). Digital twins are a bridge to industry the audience may already know.
